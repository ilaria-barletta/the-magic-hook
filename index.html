What you need to know before starting

Tools

  • Yarn:To start, pick something thick, acryic or wool, in a bright color.
  • Hook:Every hook has a number and each yarn has a label. Pick an hook that matches the number recommended on label. Or bigger.
  • Scissors:Online you'll find amazing arts&crafts scissors. Some are even shaped like birds. The truth is that any scissor, will do the job.
  • Stitch markers:They are sold in little boxes and are really useful when you start not to skip chains or even when you know more, but the pattern is triky
  • Measuring tape:It will come handy when you need to check the lenght of your scarves, the width of your jumpers or to see how many chains fit in, let's say 30 cm.
  • Wool needles:These look a lot like normal needles but are bigger and there is no pointed tip. You'll need one to refine your work.

Stitches

  • Chain:This is the foundation of every work. Learning how to chain is the first step.
  • Single crochet:This is the most basic stitch. You'll find that most amigurumis are built using single crochet or SC for short.
  • Half double crochet: This is a stitch that will add some height to the single crochet. We'll refer to this as HDC.
  • Double crochet: This will give you a stitch even higher than the half-double crochet.We'll call this DC.
  • Magic ring:We use this when we work in circle.
  • Increase: this means you simply place 2 stitches in the same chain stitch
  • Decrease: this means you take 2 stitches at the same time on the hook and then pull through both of them as if they were one.
  • Slip stitch: It's used when we want to change color or when we finish a work. We'll call it SLST for short.
